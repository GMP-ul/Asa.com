Why Finding Lost Items is So Hard
Finding lost items at CSU is difficult because the current process is manual and uncoordinated. Without a centralized digital platform, students must rely on informal social media posts or physical bulletin boards. This lack of a unified system leads to poor categorization of items, confusion across different campus offices, and significant delays in returning property to its rightful owners.

Features

Centralize Digital Hub
Instead of checking multiple offices or scattered social media posts, ASA provides a single, official platform where all lost and found records are managed.
Easy Reporting with Visuals
Users can quickly submit "Lost" or "Found" reports that include detailed descriptions and multi-image uploads, making it much easier to identify and verify specific items like keys, wallets, or electronics.
Smart Filtering and Search
Our system features a categorized search and filter engine, allowing you to narrow down results by item type and specific details rather than scrolling through endless unorganized posts.
Real-Time Status Updates
You can track the status of an item in real-time—whether it is "Pending" or has already been "Claimed"—ensuring you have the most up-to-date information at your fingertips.
Collaborative Community Effort
ASA brings together the entire CSU community, including students, faculty, staff, LSG officers, and campus security, to work as a unified network for returning lost property to its rightful owners.
